About

I'm Luca, a Mechanical Engineering student at Kettering University. I've joined General Motors as a Manufacturing Engineering Co-op focused on Global Automation & Cybersecurity at the GM Global Technical Center. Autonomous vehicles and intelligent systems are what pulled me into engineering and specifically automotive — I've always been fascinated by how sensors, processors, and algorithms come together to make a machine that thinks. Kettering's co-op model means I'll be splitting time between the classroom and real industry work from the start, and getting that first rotation at GM feels like exactly the right beginning.

Beyond engineering I've always been drawn to complex systems of all kinds. In high school I rebuilt a stagnant Investment Club from scratch — adding real lessons on shorting, stop-losses, and technical indicators, and launching a competition where the goal was to lose the most money, because learning what not to do is more valuable than chasing wins.

Outside of all that, I spent 15 years playing competitive soccer and have been playing golf for 8 — a sport that's taught me more about patience and staying composed under pressure than almost anything else.

Always happy to connect with engineers, co-op alumni, or anyone working in automotive and manufacturing.
